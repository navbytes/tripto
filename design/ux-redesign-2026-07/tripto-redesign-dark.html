Tripto · screen redesign · dark

Dark mode isn't a colour scheme you inherit

Right now light mode is Tripto — ivory, Fraunces, amber — and dark mode is iOS: black canvas, system blue links, system red destructive. Two products. These are the same components with the night values of the same tokens: amber is the only action colour, clay is the only destructive one, and Fraunces still shows up for anything you'd call a headline.

The other finding: Settings lets you choose an avatar colour but never lets you enter a name. That one missing field is why pfn8wk7vfp appears in your greeting, your traveller chips and your share sheet.

What changed

New trip · a form that shows you what it's making
  • 1The preview is the name field. No “Trip name” input, no helper explaining that it's “the big title on your trip card” — the card is right there. Same principle as the boarding pass: the form previews its own output.
  • 2Destination first, name second. Your own data names trips after their stops — “Bangkok & Khao Yai” — so pick the stops and the name composes itself. Editable, never blocking. Multi-stop is central to the itinerary and was missing from creation entirely.
  • 3One date row instead of two, plus Not sure yet. Your importer already produces undated trips (the IndiGo booking), so the model allows it — the form should too. Defaulting to “today → today+6” is a guess that's wrong nearly every time.
  • 4Trip type is cut. Its own helper admitted it: “Tripto tailors its defaults as more features arrive.” Who's coming answers family-vs-solo and does real work — it pre-fills the traveller filter on the itinerary from minute one.
  • 5Booking email as an alternate entry point. Half the time the reason you're creating a trip is that a confirmation just landed.
  • 6Create is live from the start. A sheet that opens with a dead button and “Enter a trip name to create the trip” is a form telling you off before you've done anything. An empty trip is a perfectly good trip.
  • 7Cover derives from the destination, with a shuffle to re-roll — rather than three unexplained circles.

What changed

Share · three link products became one question
  • 1People first. The screen's job is “who's on this trip and what can they do”. A link is the mechanism, not the feature — so the list leads and the invite is the primary action.
  • 2“Father — No account yet — Can be assigned plans · no app needed” was three fragments explaining one thing. It's a role: Traveller. A name on the trip, plans can be assigned, no access. Say it once, in the same control as every other role.
  • 3Four roles, one control. The chip is the picker — changing it to Companion or Viewer is what sends the invite. No separate invite button per row. Roles differ by icon and label, not by four new colours (amber stays reserved for attention — here, the waiting invite).
  • 4The public page was the loudest thing on screen, but inviting your spouse is the common case. Flip the weight. It becomes a switch with a URL, a copy, and — new — a revoke. You could create that link but never take it back.
  • 5“Your role: Organizer” card cut. It's a chip on your own row, which the list already had.
  • 6Email import moved off this screen. Getting data in and getting people in are different jobs — it's here only because both involve an address. It belongs beside “Paste a booking email” on the Add sheet: same feature, two input methods. The AI disclosure travels with it, and gets plainer: forwarded mail is read by an AI service to find bookings, then deleted.

What changed

Settings · the missing field, and a theme that forgot it had a brand
  • 1Your name. The app lets you pick an avatar colour but never asks what you're called — so it falls back to the Apple relay ID. One text field kills pfn8wk7vfp in the greeting, the traveller chips and the share sheet at once.
  • 2Home timezone belongs here. The itinerary does real timezone work — “clocks go back 1h”, what counts as today — and every bit of it needs a baseline. Plus 12/24h, since the whole app is a timetable.
  • 3“Copy conversion prompt” was the third blue link under a four-line paragraph. Bring your history from any other app via an LLM is the most distinctive idea in this build — it gets to be a feature, with a name a person would say out loud.
  • 4Export says what it exports. “20 trips · 67 items” is the difference between trusting a backup and hoping.
  • 5Account demoted below your data. pfn8wk7vfp@privaterelay.appleid.com is a credential, not an identity — it shouldn't be the second thing the app tells you about yourself.
  • 6Delete is below a rule, with no card fill — a filled row reads as an invitation. And it answers the question people actually have: what happens to the trips other people are on?
  • 7No system blue, no system red. Amber is the only action colour and clay the only destructive one, in both themes.

Open questions

  • Delete account. The copy promises “trips other people are on stay with them”. Is that true? If you're the Organizer and your spouse is a Companion, does the trip transfer, orphan, or vanish? Decide before that sentence ships.
  • Public link expiry. A read-only family itinerary that lives forever is a slow leak. Auto-expire it, say, 30 days after the trip ends — with a nudge if someone's still opening it.
  • Traveller → Companion. When a name on the trip finally gets an account, do the plans already assigned to them follow?
  • Theme. Does the app follow the system, or should there be an in-app switch? Worth deciding now, because right now light and dark are two different products.
  • Trip type. I cut it. If you're keeping it for analytics, infer it from who's on the trip rather than asking.
